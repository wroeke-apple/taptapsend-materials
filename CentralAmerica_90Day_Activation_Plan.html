00 · Strategic Framing

This Is Activation, Not Entry

The Role I'm Proposing

When Saad asked what role I'd build, I said: the person responsible for growing Central America. Not GM of LatAm — that's a different job. This is a focused growth mandate: own El Salvador, Guatemala, and Honduras, prove the unit economics, build the community-led GTM playbook, and earn the expanded mandate. TapTapSend already holds 48 US state licenses and is live in all three markets. The infrastructure exists. The question is why the volume isn't there — and how to fix it.

Central America's remittance corridor is the most structurally underpenetrated segment in TapTapSend's licensed universe. The US sends ~$30B annually to El Salvador, Guatemala, and Honduras combined — roughly 40% of all US-to-LatAm flows — yet digital penetration lags behind Mexico by 8–10 percentage points. The three reasons: distribution is still grocery/corner store, trust is fragmented, and incumbents (Western Union, MoneyGram) hold community-level relationships that fintech apps have not replicated.

I grew up in those community channels. My father sent money to El Salvador at the Western Union counter at the grocery store. I still send to my grandmother in a rural town outside San Salvador today. The trust and distribution gap in this corridor is not abstract to me — it's the thing I'd be uniquely positioned to close. Below is how I would do it in 90 days.

02 · Go-to-Market Strategy

GTM Framework: Sender-First, Community-Activated

The conventional fintech GTM for remittances is digital-first, performance-marketing-driven: Facebook/Instagram ads, app store optimization, referral codes. This works in urban corridors with high smartphone penetration. It does not work as the primary channel for Central American diaspora, where: (a) senders skew 30–55, lower digital nativity, (b) the trusted transaction venue is still the grocery store or Walmart Money Center, and (c) word-of-mouth from a trusted community member is worth 10x any digital ad impression.

The GTM strategy runs two parallel tracks.

Track 1: Sender-Side Activation (US Diaspora Communities)

Community Anchor Partnerships

Partner with 5–10 high-trust institutions in top sender metros (LA, Houston, DC/MD/VA, NYC, Dallas) — Salvadoran community organizations (e.g., CARECEN), Guatemalan hometown associations (HTAs), Honduran mutual aid networks. Provide co-branded collateral, in-person activation events, and referral economics shared with the organization.

Latino Grocery Store Network

Negotiate placement agreements with 50–100 independent Latino grocery stores in top-10 sender metros. QR code at checkout, in-store signage, staff incentive ($2–3 per first-time sender activated). This replicates the Western Union placement model at 1/10th the cost and with a trusted community endorsement baked in.

Church & Religious Network

Catholic parishes with Salvadoran/Guatemalan/Honduran congregations are the most trusted institution in diaspora communities. Bulletin inserts, post-Mass activation tables, clergy endorsement. Pilot in 10 parishes in LA and Houston in Days 1–30.

Diaspora Creator Program

Identify 20–40 micro-influencers (5K–50K followers) from the Salvadoran, Guatemalan, and Honduran diaspora on TikTok/Instagram/YouTube. Performance-based: $50 flat + $10 per first-time send. Target CPA $25–35, with community authenticity that paid ads cannot replicate.

Track 2: Recipient-Side Activation (CA3 Markets)

Payout Rail Optimization

Within 30 days: audit and expand payout options in El Salvador (Banco Agrícola, Davivienda, BAC Credomatic) and Guatemala (Banrural, G&T Continental). Target: 85%+ of recipients can receive via their primary institution. Mobile wallet integration (Tigo Money, Claro Pay) for rural coverage where bank penetration drops to 15–20%.

Recipient Notification & App Download Loop

When a recipient receives their first transfer via TapTapSend, trigger a WhatsApp message (primary communication channel in CA3) with transfer confirmation + link to recipient-facing app. Offer: "Check your balance, get notified instantly, and share TapTapSend with your sender." This seeds the reverse referral loop.

GTM Channel Mix — Target CAC & Volume Contribution (Year 1)

Sequencing Logic

El Salvador activates first (Days 1–45): dollarized, highest trust in digital post-Chivo, my field research base. Guatemala activates second (Days 46–75): largest absolute TAM, higher volume ceiling, more distribution complexity. Honduras activates third (Days 76–90 scoping, Month 4+ execution): highest rural concentration, requires mobile wallet-first approach, longer timeline.

The Neobank Multiplier Thesis

My Lauder field research found that only ~3% of remittance recipients in El Salvador keep funds in a bank account, despite 60% of flows arriving via bank deposit. This is not a product failure — it is a trust and access failure. The first financial product a recipient trusts after remittances is the one they use for everything else. TapTapSend has a structural opportunity to be that product, which is why the neobank LTV multiplier (1.8–2x) is not theoretical — it is how Nubank built to 7x revenue multiple vs. Remitly's 2.5x.

04 · 90-Day Activation Plan

Day-by-Day Execution Framework

Three phases, each with a distinct mandate and clear deliverables. Each phase gates the next: if Day 30 diagnostics reveal a payout rail gap or a CAC assumption is broken, Phase 2 priorities shift accordingly.

Days 1–30

Diagnose & Design

  • Sender audit: Pull cohort data on existing CA3 senders — frequency, avg ticket, drop-off points, re-send rate. Identify where the funnel leaks.
  • Payout rail audit: Map every bank/wallet option in SV, GT, HN. Identify gaps vs. top 5 recipient institutions.
  • Competitive pricing sweep: Mystery shop Western Union, MoneyGram, Remitly, Wise on 5 CA3 corridors. Map full fee + FX spread picture.
  • Community mapping: Identify 20 target community orgs/churches/grocery stores in LA + Houston. Schedule meetings weeks 3–4.
  • Metric baseline: Lock CAC, LTV, send frequency, and D30/D90 retention baselines from current cohorts.
  • GTM brief: Draft full channel strategy, creative brief, and partnership term sheet templates by Day 28.
Days 31–60

Pilot & Calibrate

  • El Salvador go-live: Activate 5 grocery store partnerships + 3 church pilots in LA. Measure CAC daily vs. target $20.
  • Creator program launch: Onboard 10 micro-influencers. Brief on authentic storytelling (not ad reads). First content live by Day 40.
  • Payout expansion: Close at least 2 new payout partnerships in SV (mobile wallet priority — Tigo Money or equivalent).
  • Guatemala design: Based on SV learnings, adapt GTM brief for Guatemala. Begin community org outreach in Houston + Dallas.
  • WhatsApp notification loop: Ship recipient notification feature. Track app download rate from notification link.
  • Weekly CAC review: Every Monday, review channel-level CAC. Kill any channel running >2x target. Double down on channels <50% of target.
Days 61–90

Scale & Build Roadmap

  • Guatemala activation: Launch sender-side community program in Houston + Dallas. Target 500 new GT senders in first 30 days post-launch.
  • Honduras scoping: Complete regulatory and payout rail assessment. Design mobile wallet-first GTM. Recruiting for in-country ops.
  • Neobank/card partnership: Identify 2–3 potential BIN sponsors or neobank partnerships in SV (Banco Agrícola digital arm, Chivo successor infrastructure). Draft term sheet.
  • 90-day results readout: CAC by channel, LTV:CAC ratio, cohort D30/D60/D90 retention, GTV vs. target. Present to leadership with Year 1 forecast update.
  • Year 1 plan: Finalize budget, headcount, and corridor expansion sequence for board approval.

Year 2 Unlock

The real value is not Year 1 revenue — it is the cohort quality. If Day 90 retention is above 45% and community channels drive 35%+ of acquisition, we have built a defensible, low-CAC growth engine. By Year 2, that cohort compounds: referrals from Year 1 senders become the cheapest acquisition channel, and 10–15% of Year 1 recipients convert to a card/savings product, unlocking the neobank LTV multiplier. The Year 2 bull case revenue is $8–12M, with contribution margins turning positive.
